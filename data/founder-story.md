# SetVenue — Founder Story

SetVenue came out of a simple observation: production teams were forced to use three different platforms for one project — one for locations, one for crew housing, one for events. Each had different fees, different workflows, different problems.

For producers, this meant wasted time coordinating across platforms. For hosts, it meant paying 15-25% in fees to list their properties. The system was broken.

SetVenue was built to create a better default.

---

## The Problem

The production booking process is fragmented. Producers piece together locations from Giggster, housing from Airbnb, and events from Peerspace. That creates friction at every step:

- Multiple platforms, logins, and invoices
- Fees ranging from 15-25% across platforms
- Generic rental platforms not built for productions
- Hosts rejecting commercial use
- No unified workflow for production teams

---

## The Solution

SetVenue addresses this by offering a unified platform for production bookings — locations, stays, and events in one place.

- **10% fee** vs 15-25% competitors
- **Production-first design** — built for how productions actually work
- **One platform** — locations, housing, events
- **0% host fees** — only guests pay

---

## The Vision

The long-term vision is to become the go-to platform for production teams worldwide.

- A marketplace brand that feels credible, production-focused, and operationally useful
- Lower fees that competitors can't easily match
- Network effects as more productions use the platform
- Infrastructure for the production economy

---

## Why Now

Production teams are tired of:
- Paying 15-25% fees
- Juggling multiple platforms
- Generic rental platforms that don't understand productions
- Hosts who reject commercial bookings

SetVenue is not trying to be a generic marketplace with a new coat of paint. The point is specificity.

It is built for production teams who need locations, housing, and events — and want one platform that actually works for them.
