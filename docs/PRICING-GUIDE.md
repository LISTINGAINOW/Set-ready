# SetVenue Pricing Guide

**Purpose:** Help property owners set competitive rates. Reference for agents and Josh when asked "what should I charge?"

---

## How SetVenue Pricing Works

- Owner sets their price (hourly, daily, or monthly)
- Owner keeps 100% of that price
- Renter pays owner's price + 10% SetVenue service fee
- Example: Owner lists at $500/day → Renter pays $550 → Owner gets $500, SetVenue gets $50

## What Affects Pricing

**Charge more if:**
- Unique/iconic architecture
- Ocean, mountain, or skyline views
- Pool, rooftop, or large outdoor space
- Recently renovated/modern
- Privacy (gated, no neighbors visible)
- Close to production hubs (studios, agencies)
- Lots of natural light
- Parking for 5+ vehicles

**Charge less if:**
- Standard suburban home
- Small spaces
- Limited parking
- Noise restrictions
- HOA rules limiting crew size/hours
- Far from city center

## What to Tell Owners

### "What should I charge?"

"It depends on your property and market. Here's what similar spaces in your area are getting:

[Reference the table above for their property type + city]

I'd suggest starting at the mid range and adjusting based on demand. You can always raise your rate if you're getting booked fast, or lower it if inquiries are slow.

The beauty is you set the price — we never dictate what you charge."

### "Can I charge different rates for different types of bookings?"

"Absolutely. Many owners charge:
- Hourly for photo shoots (quick, low impact)
- Daily for film productions (full day, more crew)
- Monthly for extended projects
- Flat rate for events

You can set different pricing tiers on your listing."

### "What if someone tries to negotiate?"

"That's between you and the renter. Some owners are firm on price, others negotiate for longer bookings or repeat clients. We recommend setting a minimum you're comfortable with and sticking to it."
