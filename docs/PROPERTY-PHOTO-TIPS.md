# Property Photo Tips

**Purpose:** Help owners take better listing photos. Send this link when someone's photos are weak. Better photos = more bookings.

---

## The Basics

Production companies are visual people. They decide in 3 seconds whether your property is worth clicking on. Photos are everything.

**Minimum:** 10 photos to list on SetVenue
**Sweet spot:** 20-35 photos
**Hero shot:** Your single best photo appears in search results. Make it count.

## What to Photograph

### Must-Have Shots
- [ ] Wide exterior (show the full property, ideally golden hour)
- [ ] Front entrance
- [ ] Living room (widest angle possible)
- [ ] Kitchen (clean counters, nothing on them)
- [ ] Master bedroom
- [ ] Master bathroom
- [ ] Every additional bedroom
- [ ] Backyard/patio/deck
- [ ] Pool (if applicable)
- [ ] View (ocean, mountain, city — if you have one, show it)
- [ ] Driveway/parking area (crews need to park vans)
- [ ] Street view (shows neighborhood feel)

### Bonus Shots That Win Bookings
- Staircase (cinematic staple)
- Hallways (camera movement paths)
- Windows with natural light streaming in
- Unique architectural details
- Fireplace
- Garage (can double as staging area)
- Aerial/drone shot (if possible)
- Night shot with lights on (moody, cinematic)

## How to Shoot

### Lighting
- **Shoot during golden hour** (first hour after sunrise or last hour before sunset)
- **Turn on ALL lights** in the house — even during the day
- **Open ALL curtains and blinds** — natural light is king
- **No flash** — it flattens everything and creates harsh shadows

### Composition
- **Shoot from corners** — aim diagonally across rooms to make them look bigger
- **Keep the camera at chest height** — not eye level, not floor level
- **Landscape orientation** (horizontal) for everything except tall features
- **Include two walls and the floor** in every room shot — shows scale
- **Shoot through doorways** — creates depth

### Staging
- **Declutter everything** — less is more
- **Remove personal items** — family photos, kids' toys, medications
- **Remove toilet seats up, trash cans visible, pet items**
- **Make beds, fluff pillows, fold towels**
- **Add fresh flowers or a fruit bowl** on the kitchen counter
- **Hide cables and electronics**
- **Clean windows** — they'll be in every shot

### Common Mistakes
- ❌ Bathroom selfie in the mirror
- ❌ Dark rooms with no lights on
- ❌ Portrait (vertical) photos of rooms
- ❌ Clutter on counters and tables
- ❌ TV on in the background
- ❌ Finger over the lens (yes, it happens)
- ❌ Only 3-4 photos (minimum 10)
- ❌ All photos of the same room

## Phone Camera Tips

You don't need a professional camera. A modern iPhone or Android is fine.

- Use the **wide angle lens** (0.5x) for room interiors
- Use the **standard lens** (1x) for details and exteriors
- **Wipe the lens** before shooting (fingerprints kill sharpness)
- Use **HDR mode** (usually auto) for high-contrast scenes
- Hold the phone **with both hands** or use a tripod
- **Take 3 shots of each angle** — pick the best one later

## For Production Companies Looking at Listings

They're evaluating:
- Can I fit a camera, lights, and crew in this room?
- Is there good natural light?
- Is the style right for my project?
- Where does the crew park?
- Where do we stage equipment?
- What's the neighborhood like? (quiet? permits needed?)

Your photos should answer all of these without the renter having to ask.
