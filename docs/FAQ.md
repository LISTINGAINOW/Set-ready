# SetVenue FAQ

**Purpose:** Answer common questions from both property owners and production companies. Will eventually live on the site.

---

## For Property Owners

### How much does it cost to list?
Nothing. We charge the renter a 10% service fee. You keep 100% of your listed price. For our first 500 properties, listing is free for 6 months.

### How do I set my price?
You choose your own rate — hourly, daily, or monthly. Check our Pricing Guide for typical ranges in your area. You can change your price anytime.

### What kind of productions will book my property?
Film crews, photographers, brand campaigns, music videos, corporate events, podcasts, social media content creators. It depends on your space — modern kitchens attract food shoots, big backyards attract events, unique architecture attracts everything.

### Do I have to be home during the shoot?
That's up to you. Some owners are present, others give access and leave. You set the rules on your listing.

### What about damage?
Production companies are required to carry production insurance. You approve every booking before it happens, and you can require a security deposit. We also have a dispute resolution process for any issues.

### Can I block certain dates?
Yes. You control your availability calendar. Block any dates you want.

### How do I get paid?
Payments go through Stripe. After a confirmed booking, payment is held until the booking is complete, then released to you on Stripe's standard payout schedule (2-7 business days).

### Can I list on other platforms too?
Absolutely. No exclusivity required. Most owners list on multiple platforms. Just keep your calendar updated to avoid double bookings.

### What if I don't like a booking request?
You approve or decline every request. No obligation to accept anyone. If something feels off, just decline.

### Do I need a business license or permit?
It depends on your city. Some cities (like LA) require a TOT license for short-term rentals. Check your local regulations. Our listings include compliance fields where you can display your license info.

### How long does it take to get my first booking?
Honest answer: it varies. Properties with great photos in high-demand areas (LA, Atlanta, NYC) tend to get inquiries faster. We're actively bringing production companies to the platform.

## For Production Companies

### How does pricing work?
The owner sets their price. You pay that price plus a 10% SetVenue service fee. That's it. No hidden charges.

### How do I book a property?
Browse listings, find a space that fits, submit a booking request with your dates and production details. The owner reviews and approves. Once approved, you pay through the platform.

### Can I tour the property first?
Reach out to the owner through the platform to arrange a site visit. Most owners are happy to show their space before committing.

### What types of properties are available?
Luxury homes, estates, lofts, warehouses, restaurants, rooftops, ranches, commercial spaces, and more. Filter by city, type, features, and budget.

### What cities do you cover?
Currently: Los Angeles, Atlanta, New York City, Austin, Miami, and Nashville. More cities coming soon.

### What's your cancellation policy?
Each property has its own cancellation policy (Flexible, Moderate, Strict, or Custom). Check the listing details before booking. Full policies at setvenue.com/legal/cancellation.

### Do I need production insurance?
Yes. All renters are required to carry production liability insurance. This protects both you and the property owner.

### Can I book for multiple days?
Yes. Many properties offer daily, weekly, and monthly rates. Extended productions often get better rates — contact the owner to discuss.

### How is this different from Giggster or Peerspace?
Our fees are lower (10% vs 15-25%), we cover locations + events + extended stays in one platform, and we have a growing inventory of unique properties that aren't listed anywhere else.

### Who do I contact if there's a problem?
Email us or use the dispute resolution process at setvenue.com/legal/disputes. We respond within 24 hours.
